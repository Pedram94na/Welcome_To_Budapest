Summary

Budapest has a rich and fascinating history that spans over 2,000 years. The area that is now Budapest was first settled by the Celts in the 1st century BC, and later became part of the Roman Empire. In the 9th century AD, the Magyars (ancestors of modern-day Hungarians) established their own kingdom in the region. The city of Budapest itself was formed in 1873, with the unification of Buda and Pest, which were previously separate cities on opposite sides of the Danube River. In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, Budapest became a cultural and intellectual hub of Europe, with a thriving arts scene and numerous universities. Budapest suffered greatly during World War II, with significant damage to its buildings and infrastructure. The city was later under Soviet control for several decades, which had a profound impact on its political and cultural landscape. Since the fall of communism in 1989, Budapest has undergone significant changes and modernization. Today, it is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city with a rich cultural heritage, attracting millions of visitors each year.
